What you get

  • Adaptive targets. Most apps hand you one calorie number and never change it. Ignia recalibrates from your own weight trend and intake, so progress doesn't stall.
  • A real training log. Workout templates, sets/reps/RIR, double-progression suggestions, plate calculator, warm-up generator. No other macro tracker ships this.
  • Fast logging. Scan a barcode, search USDA FoodData Central and Open Food Facts, type a meal in plain language, or tap a saved preset.
  • An AI coach grounded in your own logs — not generic advice.
  • The rest of the picture. Fasting timer, body weight and measurements, body-fat estimate, weekly insights, weight-trend projection.
  • Your data stays yours. Import from MyFitnessPal, Lose It! or Cronometer; export to CSV anytime; delete your account from inside the app.
  • English and Spanish (Puerto Rico), fully translated.

Common questions

Is it really free?
Yes. Every feature is free. There's an optional tip if you want to support development — it unlocks nothing, because there's nothing locked.
Are there ads?
No ads, no data selling, no cross-site tracking. See the privacy policy.
When is the Android version out?
In testing. Until then the web app works well on Android — open ignia.fit/app in Chrome and choose "Add to Home screen".
Can I move my history over from another app?
Yes — Settings → Data imports MyFitnessPal, Lose It! and Cronometer CSV exports.
How is this different from MyFitnessPal or MacroFactor?
MyFitnessPal gives you a static target and no training log. MacroFactor has adaptive targets but no training log, and costs about $72/year. Ignia does both, free. Full comparisons: vs MyFitnessPal, vs MacroFactor, vs Lose It!, vs Cronometer.
